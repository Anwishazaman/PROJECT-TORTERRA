Sandy Soil

This kind of soil usually gets washed away during heavy rains, often carry fewer nutrients for the plants because it warms up faster. It is easier to cultivate this soil and it drains and dries out fast.

Learn More

Clay Soil

This soil is poor at water-drainage and allows fewer air spaces, it is heavier to cultivate and gets warmed up slowly during the spring season. This soil is hard and rocky when dry and gets sticky and lumpy when it is wet.

Learn More

Loamy Soil

This soil is a mix of silt, clay, and sand, it is damp and fine-textured soil. This soil can be easily cultivated and warms up easily but does not become too dry during summer months. This is an ideal soil for outdoor plants. Learn More

Peaty Soil

This soil offers fewer nutrients because the acidic nature of the soil slows the decomposition process, high amounts of peat lend spongy and damp qualities to this dark soil. This soil heats up easily and can retain water.

Silt Soil

This offers great benefits to the plants if the drainage system is channelized properly, it tends to hold moisture and has a soapy and soft texture to it. This soil is comparatively easier to cultivate.
